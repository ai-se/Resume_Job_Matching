Job Title = Engineer

Overview

Bechtel Plant Machinery, Inc. (BPMI) is seeking a resident engineer at a supplier location in Batavia, New York.

Responsibilities

* Perform quality and manufacturing over-site through shop surveillance, oversight with DCMA, production follow, and cause and corrective action reviews.

* Measure manufacturing progress against contract milestones and schedules, assess reasons for any delays, and work with the supplier and BPMI personnel to recover to the established schedule.

* Lead and contribute to the identification and resolution of both quality and production issues.

* Assess the effectiveness of supplier initiatives to improve quality and delivery performance and provide feedback and recommendations to management.

* Assess production control processes as well as shop resources and critical operation capacity.

* Recommend and execute plans to continually improve surveillance performance.

* Team with supplier to perform surveillance at critical sub-tiers to assess and/or identify issues, monitor progress, and assess capability.

* Communicate effectively with individuals representing a wide range of corporate levels from shop personnel to senior management across multiple organizations.

* Formally document and communicate key oversight activities with stakeholders.

Qualifications

* B.S. in Mechanical Engineering preferred from an accredited college or university. Other engineering fields will be considered with relevant experience.

* Minimum GPA of 3.0 is preferred.

* Minimum of 2 years experience.

* Experience in quality, manufacturing, or supplier management.

* Ability to communicate effectively across multiple levels of an organization.

* Ability to work independently with limited direction.

* Ability to work safely in a shop environment.

* Must be a U.S. citizen due to government security clearance requirements. In order to work at BPMI, applicants must complete a Federal background investigation and be granted a security clearance.

BPMI is a prime contractor for the United States Naval Nuclear Propulsion Program (NNPP). We are a project engineering organization responsible for providing design, manufacturing, and procurement management for the fabrication, testing, delivery, installation, and field support of high quality nuclear power plant components for installation in submarines and aircraft carriers.
