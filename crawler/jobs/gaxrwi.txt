Job Title = Machinist

Machinist Charlotte, NC Tech USA is currently seeking a Machinist for a full-time position with an established company in the greater Charlotte area.
Purpose of this Position The primary function of the Machinist is to safely use advanced machining equipment to produce test specimens, fixtures and/or additional items required to meet shop schedules and needs.
Essential Duties and Responsibilities: -Safely operate and maintain CNC Mills/CNC Lathes/CNC grinders.
-Meets all Safety, Health and Environmental, Quality and production requirements in accordance with established policies and procedures.
-Ensures that equipment is maintained and ready for use, to include, but not limited to routine/advanced maintenance, calibration, and tooling condition verification.
-Insures adequate supplies are available to meet operational needs.
-Complete the necessary paperwork and computer entries.
-Effectively prioritize work load and use of available resources within time constraints.
-Clean up the machine and working area at the end of the shift.
Education and/or Experience An Associates Degree in manufacturing or related discipline from a Technical College is preferred.
Qualification as a machinist and at least 1 year of experience in a machine shop setting is preferred.
Ability to read/work from prints and hand drawn sketches and experience in using the machinist handbook are required.
Language Skills Ability to read and interpret documents such as safety rules, operating and maintenance instructions, customer testing specifications, and procedure manuals.
Mathematical Skills Ability to apply concepts of basic algebra and geometry.
Reasoning Ability Ability to solve practical problems and deal with a variety of concrete variables in situations where only limited standardization exists.
Ability to interpret a variety of instructions, data and details furnished in written, oral, diagram, or schedule form.

-Ability to remain calm under pressure of deadlines and multiple priorities.
-Flexibility with schedules.
-Working knowledge of computers.
-Working knowledge of the machinery to enable troubleshooting small problems.
-Working knowledge of micrometers, calipers and other measurement tools.
