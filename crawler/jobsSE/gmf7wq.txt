
The Senior Cyber Security Engineer (Penetration Tester) will evaluate the security of Open Text infrastructure by continuously assessing and exploiting vulnerabilities to identify where hacking threats may reside. This is a highly technical hands on role that that is responsible for emulating advanced persistent threat (APT) and other sophisticated cyber attackers to perform internal/external attack testing, planning and coordinating tests that mimic known adversarial tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs). You are great at:
•Planning and conducting attacks on internal and external networks and applications•Creating tests to identify vulnerabilities across the infrastructure (systems, networks, applications) to pinpoint entry points for hackers•Designing and developing scripts, frameworks, tools and the methods required for facilitating and executing complex attacks and emulating adversarial TTPs•Performing malware analysis and malware reverse engineering to extract indicators of compromise to be used to support testing and hunting activities•Performing in-depth research of the latest adversarial TTPs and technology•Providing support for audits and gathering of artifacts for ISO27001, PCI, SOC1 & SOC2, etc.•Developing comprehensive and accurate reports and presentations for both technical and executive audiences •Communicating findings and strategy to peers and management•Providing training to security engineers and analysts on attack techniques, tool/exploit development, intelligence analysis and adversarial tactics
